My Journey


"My career began supporting homeless adults as a Service Broker in 2007 before earning my Social Work MSc in 2014 and transitioning into Child Protection, where I worked directly with children, young people, and families facing complex challenges.

In 2017, I joined YMCA DLG as their Boys and Young Men's Worker, specializing in supporting males at risk of sexual exploitation and those displaying harmful sexual behaviour. As a WiSE Schools Liaison Worker (2023-2024), I partnered with schools across Brighton & Hove to tackle harmful sexual behaviour through targeted staff training and practical resources—experience that directly informs my training approach today.

I continue to have a passion for addressing the often-overlooked health and social needs of boys and young men, including mental health support, violence prevention, and the critical need for male-specific approaches. "
